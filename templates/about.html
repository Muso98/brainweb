Platform purpose

BrainWeb is a closed clinical tool that integrates AI models into everyday neuroimaging workflow. The platform does not replace radiologists; instead it provides fast, reproducible measurements and visualisations that can support expert interpretation of brain tumours.

  • Upload brain MRI/CT studies in anonymised form.
  • Run AI-assisted tumour detection and segmentation.
  • Inspect slice-by-slice overlays inside the viewer.
  • Export a structured PDF report with volumes and diameters.

Who can use BrainWeb?

For patient safety and data protection, access is limited to authorised clinical staff and approved research collaborators. Public self-registration is intentionally disabled. Anonymous visitors cannot:

  • upload or download any imaging data;
  • view dashboards, AI results or patient information;
  • use the built-in AI assistant with clinical context.
Important: if you are not registered in the system, you will not be able to use BrainWeb. First-time visitors should read this page and contact the administrator to request an account.

How to request access

To obtain a BrainWeb account, please contact your local system administrator or PACS coordinator. Your role (radiologist, neurosurgeon, resident, researcher, etc.) will determine which features you see.

  • Send an email from your institutional address.
  • Specify your full name, department and role.
  • Mention whether you will use BrainWeb for clinical work or research.

After approval, you will receive login credentials or information on how to use your institutional single sign-on (if enabled).
